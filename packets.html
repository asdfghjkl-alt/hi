Packets and Routing

What are protocols?

A protocol is a system of rules that allows electronic devices to communicate with each other. These rules judge what type of data is transmitted, and how data transfers are confirmed.

What is a packet?

Packets are units of data that is routed between a destination on the internet or other networks. Packets are often very small, being around 1.5KBs.

What is a router?

Routers are devices that forward data packets between computer networks. Routers perform the traffic directing functions on the Internet.

How does fault tolerance work?

Fault tolerant is the ability to continue an opperation of a process without interuption by any means. If your computer is not fault tolerant, small processes will probably break a component very easily

TCP

TCP is the Transmission control protocol, which ensures the reliable transmission of packets. This makes sure that information isn't corrupted or deleted.

What is scalability?

Scaleability is the ability of a computer application to continue to function well when it changes in size to meet the user's expectation.
