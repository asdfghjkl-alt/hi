The Internet

What is the Internet?

You may not know it, but the internet is not just games and websites. The internet is global, stretching across the world to deliver you, the consumer information that you may need. It is a variety of interconnected networks, which uses standardized communication protocols, such as TCP/IP.

What are the measurements of storage in a computer?

Bits, bytes, Kilobytes and Megabytes are all measurements of storage on a computer. Nower days, there are even gigabytes, but what are these in the first place?

Bits are values that can hold a value of 0 or 1. Now, with many of these bits, computers can become more and more complex, as our storage systems grow more and more advanced. These values can be combined to create some of your favourite programs like Minecraft or Chrome.

What is binary and how do you convert a number into binary?

Binary is a base-2 system, meaning that it has two possible outcomes. It was created by Gottfried Leibnizz, and is only made up out of 2 numbers. It is the fundamental of human technology.

The way that you convert a number into binary is to constantly divide the number by two, and keep a notice on the remainder. Stop once the quotient is zero, and reverse the digits.

What is decimal? (Other than maths)

Decimal describes the base 10 number system. The decimal number system consists of digits 0 - 9. It is mainly used on computer applications and each digit has a position, worth 10 times more than the previous digit.

Ethernet, Fibre Optic Cable, Wireless

All of these relate to how we connect computers on a network. Wireless connections are a fairly new thing, whereas in the 1990s, fibre optic and ethernet cords were the most widespread way to connect to a network.

Bandwidth

The maximum data transfer of a network or Internet connection. Bandwidth measures how much data can be sent over a connection in an amount of time.

Digital and Analogue

Digital are signals of data expressed as series of 0s and 1s. It is typically represented by values of a physical quantity such as voltage or magnetic polarization.

Analog is any continuous signal where the time varying feature of the signal is a representation of some other time-based variable.

What is IP?

IP is internet protocol, the set of rules governing forms of data that are sent on the internet. IP is identifiers that allows information to pass and sent between devices. It can also conatin location information as well.

IP addresses are a numerical label that is assigned to each device connected to a computer network that uses the Internet for communication.

What is DNS?

DNS is the Domain Name System, and is used for transferring alphanumeric domain names into numeric IP addresses.
